Forecasts
Unified financial forecast — AR collections, pipeline-to-revenue, and 12-month projection. Planning inputs, not alarms.
How the three forecasts interact:
• AR forecast (high confidence — already-invoiced money): each open invoice gets paired with that customer's median DSO. Auto-computed from QBO Transaction Detail. Maps to a 30/60/90/120-day bucket.
• Pipeline expected by GROUP (lower confidence — probability-weighted): pending bids split into Group A/B/C buckets (Group A wins ≥70%, Group B 30–69%, Group C historically near 0%). Each group's pending $ × that group's $-win rate. Per-group rates are much more accurate than a single blended rate — Group C pending shouldn't be valued the same as Group A pending.
• Financial projection: next-12-month revenue from last complete year's monthly shape × current YoY pace. OpEx held at prior-year monthly average.
Why AR and Pipeline are shown separately: stacking $500K of real-soon AR with $2M of probability-weighted pipeline 120+ days out makes the AR look small. They're different kinds of numbers. AR is precise and near-term; pipeline is a planning input with real uncertainty about which bids land and when.
Need at least Open Invoices, Knowify, and one complete year of P&L to render forecasts.

How this forecast is built — the full chain:
  ● Actual — months already closed; from the QBO monthly P&L.
  ● Pipeline (current pending) — for each currently-pending Knowify bid: extract GC, classify into Group A/B/C, multiply bid CV by THAT GROUP's $-win rate, time-shift by Knowify median bid-to-award lag + ~30d project duration, land the expected revenue in that month.
  ● Pipeline (forward arrivals) — for months past where current pending lands: average new-bids/month × win rate × avg CV. Captures bids that haven't arrived yet but will.
  ● Seasonal uplift — when prior-year-shape × YoY pace gives a higher number than the pipeline view (signal that historical throughput exceeds what's currently in the funnel).
Each month's revenue = max(seasonal baseline, pipeline-grounded). COGS = revenue × (1 - GM%). OpEx is split FIXED (rent, insurance, salaries, utilities — held flat) and VARIABLE (fuel, vehicle, materials, subcontractors — scale with revenue). Then per-customer DSO carries it from booked revenue → AR → cash.
