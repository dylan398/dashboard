CUSTOMER SEGMENTATION
Group A / B / C view of the GC bid book — descriptive only.
How to read this page: the groups below describe the *shape* of the bid book — which GCs win bids consistently, which are competitive, which haven't awarded SFS work despite many submissions. The segmentation came out of the April-2026 pricing-sensitivity analysis. Treat the recommendations attached to each group with significantly less weight than the groupings themselves — they're analyst suggestions, not directives.

Role split for outreach (when it happens): Group B is owner-level — relationship-keeping calls/dinners with people SFS already does business with. Group C is estimator-level — diagnostic post-loss calls to find out *why* these bids aren't turning into wins (incumbent sub? scope? certification? price?). Different conversations, different people. Don't conflate them.

PlanHub is shown separately under data-artifact: won PlanHub bids get renamed to the real GC in Knowify after award, so its apparent 0% win rate isn't a real number.

▸Group C — Zero-win subgroupsEstimator-level outreach · 152 GCs · 1,797 bids · 0 historical wins
If anyone reaches out to Group C, it's the estimators — not the owners. The relevant question is why SFS isn't getting bids: incumbent sub? Scope mismatch? Certification barrier? Price? Each subgroup below is a different starting hypothesis for that diagnostic conversation.
C-STOP chain-locked-pattern hypothesis
~22 GCs that build almost exclusively for one or two national brands (AutoZone, CVS, Brakes Plus, McDonald's via Morrison). Zero-win history through these GC paths suggests a corporate-locked preferred sub, but estimators would need to confirm. Whether to keep bidding any of them is a judgment call, not a dashboard recommendation.
C-PUB certification hypothesis
25 GCs whose bid stream is dominated by ISD, city, fire-station, and similar public-sector projects. Zero SFS wins. Most natural diagnostic question for an estimator: "Do your projects require HUB/MWBE/bonding certifications we don't hold?"
C-MIX no-clear-pattern · 103 GCs
GCs with 5+ bids and 0 wins where the job names show no clear structural pattern (Ridgemont 60 bids, Sword 36, HCI 43, …). Why these GCs never award SFS isn't visible in the data — could be incumbent subs, geographic, scope, or pricing. Estimator-level diagnostic call would ask "Who got the work and why?" Showing the top 20 by bid volume.
