Forward booking visibility — capacity & cash planning, not bid selection.
How to read this page: SFS bids every job they can — this isn't a "which leads to chase" page. It's a forward-visibility page. The top number to watch is the expected booking floor (pending bid $ × historical $-weighted win rate). That's a planning floor for crews and equipment over the next 60–90 days. Everything below it is supporting context: how fast the GC market is awarding, where pending volume currently sits, and how the data was reclassified per the SFS Knowify rules.

▸Knowify ruleset (transparency)
  1. Bidding sheet = the live competitive funnel (pending bids).
  2. Active sheet + Closed sheet w/ > $0 invoiced = wins.
  3. Rejected sheet + Closed w/ $0 invoiced + stale Bidding (>60 days old) = losses.
  4. Relationship leads (Veronica, Mona, Aaron, Joe, Larry, Tyler, Marcello, Mom — see RELATIONSHIP_LEADS in dash.js) are tagged relationship and excluded from competitive win-rate math.
  5. Multi-GC bids: the same project bid through multiple GCs is annotated, not removed. Per-GC-bid is the right unit for asking "how many shots are open"; per-project is the right unit for "how many real opportunities exist."
Source: applyKnowifyRules() in core/dash.js. The full ruleset lives there in one place — don't re-implement.
